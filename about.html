I've always loved art. My first passion was drawing—capturing the gaze in people’s eyes as they looked at something beautiful, the relaxed placement of their hands, and the subtle curve of their mouths when someone they loved entered the room. I found joy in painting too, with its vibrant colors, landscapes, and cloudscapes. Photography, for me, became an extension of this love. It allows me to capture people in ways they might not see themselves.

I began photographing brides because of my admiration for the timeless black-and-white portraits of my aunts and older sisters-in-law. A man can wear a tux anytime he wants, but a woman wears her wedding dress only once—on her wedding day. It’s a symbol of the wedding itself, and I love capturing the beauty and significance of that moment. With photography, I create visual stories—-unique paintings made of moments.

I especially love when clients bring their own playfulness and creativity to a shoot. It’s a collaboration, an artistic partnership where we create together, capturing not just a pose but an experience, a feeling. Each picture is a window, revealing a special essence, a fleeting expression, or a connection. It’s a moment preserved, forever timeless. I cherish the opportunity to document people’s memories, telling their stories in ways that feel genuine and personal.
